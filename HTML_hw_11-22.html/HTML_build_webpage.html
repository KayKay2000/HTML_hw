DOG ADOPTION

When searching for a new canine pet, many families research breeders to get a full bred puppy. Although there is nothing wrong with that, there are millions of dogs available for adoption. Here are some facts from the ASPCA:

  • According to the ASPCA, 3.1 million dogs enter an animal shelter annually.
  • Approximately 390,000 dogs are euthanized annually.
  • Approximately 2 million dogs are adopted each year.
  • About 710,000 stray dogs are returned to their owner
  • About 60% of shelter dogs are euthanized each year.

Contrary to popular belief not all dogs in animal shelters and rescue organizations have behavioral problems. Many times, people have to leave their pet(s) behind due to a relocation. Some rescue organizations also train dogs to help them get adopted quicker. Here are some facts about dogs in general and dog adoption:

  1. Adopting from a shelter or rescue organization is cheaper than buying from a breeder.
  2. Adopting helps the rescue or shelter keep rescuing other animals
  3. Owning a dop helps you live longer compared to not having a dog
  4. Not all rescue dogs are mutts, some are actually purebred
  5. You might get a dog who's very unique and mixed with many types of dogs.
  6. Rescue organizations and animal shelters actually have puppies
